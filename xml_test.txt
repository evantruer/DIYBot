								<summary type="str">Use a rod with finials if your tapestry has a rod pocket.</summary>
								<html type="str">Most modern tapestries will come with a rod pocket along the top edge. In this case you can use a rod, which will look a bit like a curtain rod. This is a simple and straightforward method for hanging tapestries.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Brackets can be added to the wall from which to hang the rod. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Keep in mind that this method will result in your tapestry hanging away from the wall, with the distance depending on your particular brackets. If you want you tapestry to hang flush with the wall, try another method.&lt;cite num=&quot;1&quot;&gt;[1]&lt;/cite&gt; &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>

								<summary type="str">Obtain a rod, finials and wall brackets.</summary>
								<html type="str">Choose a rod with decorative finials that will fit in with the room’s existing decor. Although there are rods made specifically for tapestries, you can also use normal curtain rods. However, you must make sure the rod fits inside the pocket of the tapestry. The necessary rod diameter depends upon the weight of the tapestry and the length depends on the width of the tapestry.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;A 1/2 inch to 3/4 inch diameter rod will hold an average or large size tapestry anywhere from 2 to 10 lbs. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;The rod should be long enough so that the rod finials extend beyond the edges of the tapestry with the brackets meeting the edges of the tapestry. This will allow the tapestry to slightly shadow the brackets giving more focus to the finials than the brackets. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;The wall brackets should stick out from the wall a little so that the tapestry will hang at least ½ inch (1 cm) away from the wall. This will prevent moisture build-up. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;You may have to hang the tapestry a bit higher if hanging over a sofa or if you have very high walls. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>

								<summary type="str">Iron the tapestry if needed.</summary>
								<html type="str">Prior to hanging your tapestry, ironing may be needed to remove creases. Use a low to medium iron to test first and iron on the back of the tapestry. If creases do not come out by ironing the back, then use a dye-free bed sheet and place over the front of the tapestry to iron.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;You may need to increase the temperature of the iron and use some steam to remove the creases but always test in a corner of the tapestry first. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>

Drill holes spaced so that the brackets sit just outside the width of the tapestry. Use a level to make sure these holes are even so that your tapestry does not end up crooked.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Tapestries do not always need to be hung on wall studs. In most cases you can simply use drywall screws and hang the tapestry wherever you like. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;If you do not have a level you can also measure from the ceiling down to where you would like to hang the tapestry. 								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Use this method if you have a tapestry with fringe at the top.</summary>
								<html type="str">Having fringe hanging down from the top of your tapestry may not look very appealing. This method hides the top fringe behind a board or piece of wood, creating a cleaner and more polished look. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">2</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/2/2e/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-38-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-38-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/2/2e/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-38-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-38-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/3/31/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2038.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Cut a piece of wood so that it is just shorter than the width of the tapestry.</summary>
								<html type="str">You will use a piece of wood to hold up the tapestry so the size of wood required depends on the weight of the tapestry.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;For normal applications you can use a piece of 1x2 (22mm by 44mm) or 1x3 (22mm by 72mm) wood but if your tapestry is particularly large or heavy you should consider larger sizes. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt; &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">3</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/5/58/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-39-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-39-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/5/58/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-39-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-39-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/3/32/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2039.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Paint the piece of wood.</summary>
								<html type="str">Paint the wood using to prevent acid deterioration of the tapestry where it will be touching the wood.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;You will be able to see the board on the ends so use a color that either matches the wall or the dominant colors of the tapestry. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt; &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">4</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/9/96/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-40-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-40-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/9/96/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-40-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-40-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/9/92/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2040.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Hand sew a heavy fabric along the top edge of the tapestry.</summary>
								<html type="str">Cut a heavy canvas-type fabric so that it is a few inches longer than the width of the tapestry. Hand sew this canvas to the top front edge of the tapestry.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The canvas fabric should be about six or seven inches wide, but if more support is needed for heavier tapestries then you can use a wider strip. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Make sure the stitches are straight even if the tapestry end is not perfectly straight or else you will end of with a crooked tapestry. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;You may need several rows of stitches when sewing on the canvas, especially for heavier tapestries. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Make sure each stitch covers at least two warp threads and use heavy cotton thread that matches the dominant color of your tapestry. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt; &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">5</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/7/73/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-41-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-41-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/7/73/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-41-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-41-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/4/49/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2041.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Fold over the edges of the canvas and flop this section to the back.</summary>
								<html type="str">Fold the ends of the canvas strip around to the back of the tapestry, enclosing the fringe and edges of the tapestry on the top. Then flop the entire piece over so that it is facing the back. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">6</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/c/c9/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-42-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-42-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/c/c9/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-42-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-42-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/f/f5/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2042.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Drill holes into the board or piece of wood for fastening to the wall.</summary>
								<html type="str">Drill holes near the ends of the board so that they are centered and even. They should also be equidistant from the ends. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">7</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/0/05/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-43-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-43-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/0/05/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-43-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-43-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/3/30/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2043.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Use the board to mark and drill holes into the wall.</summary>
								<html type="str">Use a level while placing the board against the wall to mark the locations you will be drilling into the wall. The best option is to use a stud finder to find studs to drill into. Alternatively you can use drywall screws and place them directly into the drywall.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Insert wall anchors if you are drilling into drywall or masonry. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Make sure the screws you will be using are strong enough if you have an especially large tapestry. Check the manufacturer’s label for their strength. &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">8</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/c/cf/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-44-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-44-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/c/cf/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-44-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-44-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/4/49/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2044.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Slide the board or painted piece of wood underneath this flap and secure with staples.</summary>
								<html type="str">Slide the painted board underneath the flap so that it is between the canvas and top of the tapestry. Use wood staples to staple the canvas directly to the backside of the piece of wood. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">9</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/e/ee/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-45-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-45-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/e/ee/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-45-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-45-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/4/48/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2045.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Lift up the hanging tapestry and secure the board to the wall.</summary>
								<html type="str">You may need help to hold up the tapestry while you are driving screws through the board and into your wall. Make sure the board is secure and level.

 &lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The canvas wrapped fringe and top edge of the tapestry should be between the board and the wall. Make sure you secure the board tightly against the wall to secure the tapestry in place. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt; &lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt;</html>
							</item>
							<item type="dict">
								<num type="str">10</num>
								<whvid type="dict">
									<lrgimg type="str">/images/thumb/6/62/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-46-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-46-preview.jpg</lrgimg>
									<smlimg type="str">/images/thumb/6/62/Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-46-preview.jpg/550px-nowatermark-Hang-a-Wall-Tapestry-Step-46-preview.jpg</smlimg>
									<vid type="str">https://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/3/31/Hang%20a%20Wall%20Tapestry%20Step%2046.360p.mp4</vid>
								</whvid>
								<summary type="str">Allow the flap of tapestry to come down once more and check that it is level.</summary>
								<html type="str">Now that the board is securely attached to the wall you can bring down the flap of tapestry that was being held up. Make sure everything looks even and straight and admire your work!</html>
							</item>

			<item type="dict">
				<heading type="str">Tips</heading>
				<type type="str">tips</type>
				<list type="list">
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">Never use adhesives when mounting fine textiles. These adhesives will damage the material.</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">You can use brads or push pins as a temporary solution for hanging your tapestry but these methods can damage the tapestry by cutting threads, putting pressure on certain locations in the weaving or rusting. Spring clips are also only a temporary solution.</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">If your tapestry still hands irregularly, consider adding a thin casing or rod pocket at the bottom. Once sewn on, you can add a rod to keep the tapestry hanging straight.</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">Always paint any wood that comes in direct contact with the tapestry. The acid in the wood can damage the textile. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">Never hang a textile with rings because the fabric’s weight will be unevenly distributed, which can damage the fabric. &lt;cite num=&quot;3&quot;&gt;[3]&lt;/cite&gt;</html>
					</item>
				</list>
			</item>
			<item type="dict">
				<heading type="str">Warnings</heading>
				<type type="str">warnings</type>
				<list type="list">
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">Always follow safety precautions when using any power tools.</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">Be very careful when using a ladder to hang tapestries in hard to reach locations. Many accidents occur every year due to falls from ladders so ensure that the ladder is secure before using.</html>
					</item>
					<item type="dict">
						<html type="str">If choosing to hang tassels with your tapestry, consider children and pets (cats) who may find tassels fun to play with. Tassels, like any other window hanging cords, can create a strangulation risk. It is best to avoid using tassels if you have kids or pets.</html>
					</item>
